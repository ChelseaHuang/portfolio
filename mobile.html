An iPad App

that would guide 30,000+ new instructional designers to develop online courses

Analysis

Problem

Due to the rapid growth of the eLearning industry and the various ways that people enter the field of e-Learning, there are inconsistencies in their knowledge of the process of creating e-Learning courses in general. Many people with different backgrounds find their way into the space of e-Learning: Some people are new to the field such as new graduates of instructional design programs; some people are experts in the field who have transitioned into a position of developing e-Learning course; and other fields such as Learning and Development and Human Resources that are in charge of orientation and onboarding are moving into the field of e-Learning to train their new recruits. The Agogos iPad app will help all of these nascent eLearning professionals learn these procedures of creating an e-Learning course.


Target Audience

New Instructional Design professionals who create eLearning courses.


Learner Personas

Jessica

Jessica just celebrated her 23rd birthday and is about to finish her last semester in the Masters program in Instructional Design at Teachers College. She landed an internship that coincides with her Integrative Project perfectly: a project lead in the Training and Development department of Company X. One of the major goals of the role will be to update existing eLearning courses to make them look and feel more current, both visually and content-wise. Specifically, Jessica is tasked to begin with a redesign their “WHY STEM?” course which aims to promote students to enter the STEM field. Although Jessica is confident that she can bring new ideas into this redesign, she is concerned that she will meet resistance from long time employees and/or trainers. Jessica has the educational theory background but she lacks exposure to Articulate and other relevant software, so she is unsure of what kind of design will be best accepted. Jessica wants to make the course look relevant and appealing to her audience through visual and interactive design while making her supervisor happy but she isn’t sure of best practices and she wants to make a good impression (of course!). Jessica is hoping to find an app where she can show the process of her thoughts to her supervisor in a clear way. She learns about Agogos from some of her classmates, who have just released a beta version into the market.

Susan

Susan is the Learning & Development is an HR department manager. She is a 35 year-old woman who has worked there for 12 years, creating the same power point presentation for onboarding new employees. Over the years, she realizes that the use of technology and eLearning is important to her work and is being pressured by higher management to incorporate technology into their training. With all of her years of experience, she wants to incorporate eLearning into the HR department’s onboarding session to make the process smoother and more engaging to new employees. Susan is not tech savvy but she knows all of the information that is needed. Susan has not had formal training in using Articulate 360 and has obtained all of her knowledge through attending conferences and doing self tutorials. She knows the basics of it and can create simple courses but they look like a PowerPoint slide with simple layer triggers. Susan attended a workshop and was intrigued by the notion of being able to incorporate game mechanics into courses, such as creating a dashboard where students can check on the progress of their onboarding training within the course without having to exit to the main menu. She realizes that there are probably many more tools in the program of which she is unaware, so she is looking for an app to help her fast track her skills and knowledge.

Ben

Ben, an itinerant 27 year-old Marketing coordinator who has been task with the sales department to work out an eLearning course on customer relations. Ben arrives at the office, grabs a coffee to start his day and once he reaches his office, he sees the pile of disorganized, outdated and confusing information in a PowerPoint deck from the sales manager. He is confused and overwhelmed with all the information. Ben was asked by his manager to lead the work in creating an effective eLearning course for the entire sales team. Ben’s manager also asked him to create a document that can be shared with other developers and receive feedback before he starts developing anything. Ben is curious and passionate about the information but likes to try to figure things out before reaching out to others because he is afraid of looking like he does not know what he is doing. Even though he knows how to use the tools to develop the courses, he does not know how to get started. He is having trouble with how to put his ideas down so that it can be understood by colleagues in the company. Ben is looking for an app that provides a basic standard procedure on how to create an eLearning design document and/or storyboard.

Test Report

For the written evaluation, we surveyed 16 people from various background as can be seen in the table above. Overall, our evaluation result is acceptable considering the time and the limitation of the prototype. People enjoyed the concept of our app and our biggest feedback was the visual design of the app. Feedback included comments such as having things pop out more for easier navigation, provide clearer objectives of the app at the beginning of the app. Some of the feedback referenced things we were already aware of in this beta version such as the development tool and setting bar not working. Some feedback we received were due to constraints of the prototype such as the drawing section being unavailable.

Future

For future iterations, we would improve our app by including the feedback that we received from our survey. Firstly, we would make the visual design of our app more cohesive. Although our first impression is a roadmap with trees, the rest of our app consists of puzzle pieces. We would pick something that is a cohesive design that better ties into the idea of “instructional design”. Secondly, we would work to expand the community and collaborative aspect of the app. To do this, we could go in two different routes. The first route is to improve on our current version of the community page and make it easily accessible within the app. The second route is to make the community page its own app. By taking the community page and making it its own app, we can then have one app that focuses on the learning aspect of instructional design and the second app focuses on the social aspect where users can share their work.
